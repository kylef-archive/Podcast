The Podcast is a show about what’s new in CocoaPods development, along with the wider Cocoa community. Hosted by CocoaPods core team members Kyle Fuller and Samuel Giddins.

Episode 1 - 2014 In Review

December 31st, 2014

This week, Kyle and Samuel introduce the show and recap what has happened in 2014. They also cover the long-awaited CocoaPods 0.36 Beta with Swift support.

We get started with the first episode by going over what’s happened in 2014.

Year in Review

Web

First of all, we run down what’s changed in the CocoaPods web properties. Including the introduction of Trunk, our search engine, and the brand new metrics API where going over the metrics for QueryKit. We then cover our new status page and then go over Orta’s redesign of CocoaDocs and talk about what’s upcoming for Swift documentation with Jazzy.

Events

There has been an outstanding amount of events hosted by the CocoaPods team over the year. Starting with The CocoaPodsAlt State of the Union, our own WWDC party. In the same week, we also hosted a Pod Roulette Hackathon (San Francisco) and CocoaPods AltLabs. We then go over one of our favourite events, the Bug Bash where Samuel first took the dive into the CocoaPods code-base.

Orta has had a busy year, he hosted a Cocoa Komittee (New York) and then various CocoaKucha’s over the world. The first one in London, then shortly after another in Berlin. He then hosted one of our favourites in San Francisco where Delisa talked about Bad Cocoa

CocoaPods

2014 has been a huge step for the tool itself, we’ve made a huge strive towards version 1.0. We talk about CocoaPods plugins and then one of the biggest features of the year per build configuration Pods in CocoaPods 0.34. Both SoundCloud and Stripe have sponsored work on CocoaPods this year. Stripe sponsored Samuel to work on the new dependency resolution algorithm Molinillo, in CocoaPods 0.35

The Changelog

A lot has changed in CocoaPods 0.36 beta.1, it shipped with the long-awaited Swift support which make’s use of Xcode frameworks. If you’re a Pod author, we urge you to check out the Pod Authors Guide to CocoaPods Frameworks to read about possible breaking changes.

Favourite Pods

This week, we go over some of the pods that we’ve been working on this year.

Kyle’s been busy pushing Swift Pod’s from back before CocoaPods could handle Swift. He talks about some of his favourites:

  • URITemplate
  • Stencil
  • QueryKit
  • PathKit

Samuel talks about RestKit, the Pod that first exposed CocoaPods to him and started his journey into open source.
